What happens to your submitted manuscript?

Introduction

Writing research articles is one of the most important parts of the academic life, given the “publish or perish” situation that every researcher must face.
However, submitting and tracking your manuscript can be a uncomfortable experience due to the complicated post-submission process. Even though journals provide online platforms for authors to check the status of their submissions, the authors may not understand what happens to their submitted manuscripts, mainly because of (1) incorrectly updated status, and (2) weird status transitions. The first situation is usually resulted from human mistakes such as the associate editor’s obliviation, the reviewers’ unpunctuality, etc. We can do nothing but to tolerate, because “to human is err.” Nevertheless, even an adept researcher may become confused with the second situation due to the lack of a systematic understanding of the peer-review process. For newbie researchers, the weird status transitions are not only confusing but also upsetting.

In this essay, I would like to give a detailed analysis of peer-review process, which helps you rationally guess what happens to your submission. I also validate my workflow diagram by analyzing several cases of “weird” status transitions.

Who handles your manuscript?

People hold different responsibilities for your submission. In general, the peer-review consists of the internal and external reviews. Editors performs the internal reviews, whereas reviews, the external ones.

Editor

An editor or editor-in-chief (EiC) decide for your manuscript. At the very beginning, the EiC roughly reviews your manuscript to determine whether it is worthy for further (i.e., external) reviews. If so, the EiC will assign an associate editor to handle your manuscript; otherwise, the EiC will reject your manuscript.

Associate editor

An associate or manage editor (AE) handles your manuscript by inviting external reviewers and making evaluation recommemdation based on the review reports. Note that the AE only makes recommendations to the EiC, who will make the final decisions based on the recommendations.

Reviewer(s)

Reviewers are invited by the AE to review your manuscript.
